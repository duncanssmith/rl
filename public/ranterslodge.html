Ranters Lodge and the Dead House lie 30 metres behind the sea defences that protect the village of Chiswell from the sea. Formerly a fishing store and a boat shed, in 1994 they were converted into two self-contained artist studios that include simple accommodation. Normally only Ranters Lodge is rented.


Artist Studio

Ranters Lodge consists of 2 floors approximately 6 x 7.5 metres. The upper floor has a sleeping platform accessed by ladder. All walls are whitewashed, the floors wood and concrete with rugs. On the ground floor there is work area with tables and a small kitchen that has a fridge, an electric cooker (baby belling) and a small microwave. On the first floor there is a further work area with a large, nearly north skylight, a day bed plus the bathroom which has a sitting bath with a shower. The sleeping platform has a 5ft mattress. Additional mattresses are available. There is central heating throughout. Outside, between the two studios is a courtyard with tables and a small barbecue.


Rental

Ranters Lodge is available to rent inclusive of heating at £130 per week, or £22 per night, (minimum two nights), except from mid June until mid September, at Christmas and at Easter when rent is £180 per week or £28 per night (minimum of two nights.)

Linen is available for an extra £6. There is also an extra charge if constant heat is required.

Please telephone Judith Frost +44(0)1305 822 096 or email jcfrost@dialstart.net for further information and availability. A 20% deposit is required to secure booking.


Access

Ranters Lodge is easily reached by public transport. Weymouth is 2 hours 40 minutes by train from London Waterloo. From Weymouth station there is a regular bus service to Portland.

Ranters Lodge is 140 miles (224km) from London and approximately 200 miles (320km) from Dover.


Location

*

Chiswell, part of the Jurassic Coast, a world heritage site, is at the eastern end of Chesil beach, an 18 mile bank of stones connecting Portland to the mainland. Portland is well known mainly for its stone, used to rebuild London after the great fire in the 17th Century. The recently closed Naval base, three prisons and several villages has meant that Portland has never been described as a rural idyll but it has many places that have an extraordinary charm and unusual beauty. West Weares, a spectacular walk where quarried stone tumbles in to the sea, is a two minute walk from the studio. There are bathing places at Church Hope cove, and on calm days in the corner of Chesil Bay. There is also diving off Chesil Beach and in Portland Harbour, windsurfing and sailing. Close to the studios is a café, a fresh fish shop and the Cove House Inn, a matter of yards away. It is a five minute walk to Fortuneswell where there is a chemist, a baker, a post office, a co-op, and an Indian and a Chinese take-away.

Portland is situated in the centre of the Dorset coastline, 4 miles from Weymouth, a traditional English seaside resort. It is with easy reach of the East Dorset Coast, including the Isle of Purbeck, which has a wild, rocky and diverse coastline, and West Dorset which has Chesil Beach, followed by fossil rich cliffs and a spectacular landscape dotted with burial mounds and earthworks.
